Markets

Three lanes, three different emotional hooks.

The same business should not be framed the exact same way in every market. Aloha Scout should localize the story instead of blasting generic copy everywhere.

Hawaii

Aloha, belonging, and place.

Lead with warmth, craftsmanship, family, hospitality, and the feeling of being known. This lane should feel grounded, respectful, and local-first.

Las Vegas Metro

9th Island energy with standout discovery.

Spotlight hidden gems, after-hours excellence, hospitality, and service businesses with personality. Less tourist cliché, more local signal.

SF Bay Area

Craft, trust, and founder substance.

In the Bay, strong positioning often comes from taste, quality, values, and credibility. Show why the business matters to the neighborhood.

MVP workflow

One client. End to end. No fake finish line.

The first Aloha Scout MVP should run one current client all the way through: research, story framing, post generation, approval, and live social posting.

1. Gather source material

Member synopsis, reviews, website, socials, owner notes, photos, video clips, and local cues.

2. Generate the content set

Instagram captions, Facebook posts, X variations, TikTok/short-video concepts, and optional video scripts.

3. Approve and publish

Kim approves the set, then the system posts to the live social channels. That publishing step is essential, not optional.

Featured angle types

The stories Aloha Scout should know how to tell.

These are the narrative frames that turn a flat business listing into something people actually stop for.

The owner story

Who built this? Why do they care? What do regulars feel when they talk about the business?

The local favorite angle

What makes this business worth noticing in its own neighborhood, not just as a generic service category?

The experience angle

How does it feel to walk in, order, book, dine, or work with them? That emotional texture matters.
